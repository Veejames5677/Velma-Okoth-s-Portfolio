Perfect Crispy French Fries

These hot chips are so crispy they stay that way even after they've gone cold. Fluffy on the inside, crispy on the outside - and stays crispy well beyond the time it takes to eat them!

Ingredients

  • 4 large russet potatoes (or other starchy potatoes)
  • 2 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • Vegetable or canola oil for frying
  • Additional salt for seasoning

Best Potato Types:

  • Australia: Sebago (common dirty brushed potatoes)
  • US: Russet (also known as Idaho potatoes)
  • UK: Maris Piper or King Edward

Instructions

1. Cut Fries

  1. Peel potatoes and cut into 6mm / ¼" thick slices.
  2. Stack 2-3 slices and cut into 6mm / ¼" thick batons.
    3. Secret Tip: Use a serrated knife! It creates a rougher surface for more crispiness.

2. Rinse

  1. Place cut fries in a colander and rinse under cold water for 15-20 seconds to remove excess surface sugars.

3. Cook in Vinegar Water

  1. Place rinsed potatoes in a pot with cold water, vinegar, and salt.
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a very gentle simmer (barely rippling surface).
  3. Cook for exactly 10 minutes.
    4. Why Vinegar? It prevents potatoes from disintegrating during simmering and helps create maximum crispiness when fried.

4. Drain and Dry

  1. Carefully remove potatoes with a spider or slotted spoon onto a colander.
  2. Spread on tea towel-lined trays to steam dry for 5 minutes.

5. First Fry (Blanching)

  1. Heat oil to 160°C/325°F in a heavy pot with at least 7cm/2.8" headroom above oil.
    2. WARNING: Oil overflow risk! Always maintain proper headroom and add fries in batches.
  2. Divide fries into 3 batches. Fry each batch for 5-6 minutes until soft but not browned.
  3. Drain on paper towels and let cool completely (at least 30 minutes).

6. Second Fry (Crisping)

  1. Increase oil temperature to 190°C/375°F.
  2. Fry in batches again for 2-3 minutes until golden brown and crispy.
  3. Drain on fresh paper towels and season immediately with salt.
Make Ahead: After first fry, you can freeze the fries. Then do the second fry directly from frozen when ready to serve!

Recipe Science

The vinegar water simmering method (developed by Kenji Lopez-Alt) is revolutionary because:

  • Washes away excess sugars that cause premature browning
  • Activates starches that create the crispy surface when fried
  • Vinegar prevents potatoes from disintegrating during the 10-minute simmer

This method produces fries that stay crispy even when cold - something rarely achieved with standard soaking/double-fry methods.
